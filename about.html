Leonie Maria Tanczer is a PhD Student at the School of History, Anthropology, Philosophy and Politics (previously: School of Politics, International Studies and Philosophy) at Queen's University Belfast (QUB) and former Fellow at the Alexander von Humboldt Institute for Internet and Society (HIIG) in Berlin. She studied Political Science (B.A.) at the University of Vienna and University of Limerick (Republic of Ireland) and Political Psychology (MSc.) at Queen's University Belfast.

Tanczer is currently working on an interdisciplinary PhD project. Her thesis includes supervision from both social sciences and engineering (ECIT) and focuses on the (in)securitisation of hacking and hacktivism. Other research interests include: Gender Studies, Cybersecurity, (Online) Collective Action and Internet Governance.

She is researching and publishing on Internet-related topics and frequently presenting at academic conferences as well as speaking at public events. Tanczer is also involved in teaching at the university level. Some of the modules she supported as Teaching Assistant include "Contemporary Europe" (2015) and "Security and Terrorism" (2016).

Tanczer is also part of the Learning Development Service (LDS) at QUB. There she provides one-to-one academic skills support for students and regularly holds workshops on topics such as essay writing, referencing or time management. Some of her presentations comprise "How to Write a Literature Review", "Methodology" or "How to Write a Result and Discussion Section".

If she is not in the "PhD Bunker", Tanczer is engaged in organising the local Cryptoparty in Belfast, trawls through the Internet for new cat videos or posts tech or feminist-related articles on Twitter. Oh, and she takes great pride in her Erd≈ës number, which is 5.
